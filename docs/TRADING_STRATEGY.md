## Entry Strategies

### Strategy 1: The Momentum Continuation

**Best for**: Trending markets

**Setup**:
1. Check 9:30 AM snapshot
2. Find stock with ALL three factors
3. Wait for 9:30-10:00 AM price action

**Entry Rules**:
- **For Longs**: Enter if price holds above 9:30 AM level
- **For Shorts**: Enter if price holds below 9:30 AM level
- **Confirmation**: Volume should remain strong

### Strategy 2: The Sector Rotation Play

**Best for**: When multiple stocks in same sector qualify

**Setup**:
1. Sector shows â‰¥3 qualifying stocks
2. Pick the strongest stock (highest % change + OI change)
3. Or pick the laggard (catching up potential)

**Entry Rules**:
- **Leader**: Enter the strongest for momentum
- **Laggard**: Enter weaker stocks expecting catch-up

**Example**:
```
Sector: NIFTY IT (Qualifying)
Stocks:
1. TCS: +2.1%, OI +15% (LEADER)
2. INFY: +1.8%, OI +12%
3. WIPRO: +1.2%, OI +11% (LAGGARD)

Strategy:
- Aggressive: Trade TCS (highest momentum)
- Conservative: Trade INFY (middle ground)
- Catch-up: Trade WIPRO (expecting to follow)
```

### Strategy 3: The OI Surge Play

**Best for**: Strong trending days

**Setup**:
1. Stock shows >20% OI increase
2. Price moving in same direction
3. Volume confirmation

**Entry Rules**:
- Enter on pullbacks (for longs) or rallies (for shorts)
- Look for 5-10 minute consolidation
- Enter when breakout resumes

**Risk**: Higher risk, higher reward strategy

### Strategy 4: The Contrarian Short Squeeze

**Best for**: Experienced traders only

**Setup**:
1. Stock falls but OI increases significantly (short buildup)
2. Price holds key support
3. Reversal candle forms

**Entry Rules**:
- Wait for reversal confirmation
- Enter when price breaks above recent high
- Quick scalp opportunity (shorts covering)

**Warning**: High risk - shorts can be right too!

## Exit Strategies

### Exit Rule 1: Time-Based

**The 2-Hour Rule**:
- Most momentum fades by 11:30 AM
- Exit 50% position by 11:00 AM
- Exit remaining by 12:30 PM

**Why**: 
- Morning momentum rarely sustains full day
- Afternoon can be choppy
- Lock in profits early

## Risk Management

### The 2% Rule

Never risk more than 2% of capital on any single trade.

```
Capital: â‚¹5,00,000
Max Risk: â‚¹10,000 per trade

If stop loss is â‚¹5 per share:
Max Shares = â‚¹10,000 / â‚¹5 = 2,000 shares
```

### Position Sizing

**Conservative** (1-2 setups/day):
- 2% risk per trade
- Max 3 positions at a time
- Total exposure: 6% of capital at risk

**Moderate** (2-4 setups/day):
- 1.5% risk per trade
- Max 4 positions
- Total exposure: 6% of capital at risk

**Aggressive** (4+ setups/day):
- 1% risk per trade
- Max 5 positions
- Total exposure: 5% of capital at risk

### Correlation Risk

**Don't trade multiple stocks from same sector**:
- If NIFTY IT is your pick, trade only 1-2 IT stocks
- Sector moves correlate - same risk, no diversification
- Better to spread across 2-3 different sectors
